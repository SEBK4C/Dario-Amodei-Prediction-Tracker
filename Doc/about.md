# About

## What this is

The **Dario Amodei Prediction Tracker** is a community research project that
collects the public, dated predictions made by Dario Amodei (CEO of Anthropic)
about artificial intelligence and Anthropic, places them on a timeline from when
they were said to when they are predicted to come true, and scores them against
observed outcomes as those dates arrive.

It is designed as a **single source of truth**: every tracked claim links back
to a primary source, and the analysis is regenerated by code from a single
dataset so the numbers are reproducible and falsifiable.

## Why

Bold AI timelines are everywhere; tracked, scored AI timelines are rare. Amodei
is an unusually concrete forecaster, which makes his statements testable. By
recording them carefully and revisiting them on a schedule, this project asks a
measurable question: **is the predicted, accelerating trajectory actually
showing up in observed events?**

## How it stays current

A daily loop re-validates the data, recomputes the statistics, flags predictions
whose windows are opening or have elapsed, and (optionally) runs a Claude-powered
research pass that searches for new statements and fresh evidence and proposes
updates via pull request. See the [Methodology](methodology.md) and the
[Changelog](changelog.md).

## Provenance & corrections

Quotes are recorded verbatim with a primary-source link; normalization of vague
horizons into dates is documented per record. If you find an error — a
misattributed quote, a wrong date, a better primary source, or a status that
should change — please open an issue or pull request against
[`Mem/predictions.yaml`](https://github.com/sebk4c/dario-amodei-prediction-tracker/blob/main/Mem/predictions.yaml).

## Disclaimer

This is an independent project. It is **not affiliated with, authorized by, or
endorsed by** Anthropic or Dario Amodei. Predictions are summarized for analysis;
always consult the linked primary source for full context. Status judgments are
the maintainers' good-faith reading of cited evidence, not official assessments.
